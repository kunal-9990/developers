Starting an engagement

At the beginning of a new client engagement, you will need to create a new OnPoint PCR engagement file. Engagement files are created and hosted on CaseWare Cloud (for more information about CaseWare Cloud, visit https://www.casewarecloud.com).

In this chapter, we will cover the following tasks:

  • Creating a new engagement file

  • Assigning staff members to the engagement

  • Sharing the engagement with your client contact

  • Setting the engagement properties

Create a new engagement file

When you are ready to begin work on a client engagement, the first thing you will do is create the new engagement file. In CaseWare Cloud, you can create your engagement file in the client entity, set key dates and budgets, and assign staff members to the file.

To create a new OnPoint PCR engagement:

  1. From the Cloud menu, select OnPoint PCR.

  2. Select the New button.

  3. In the OnPoint PCR dialog, choose your client entity and enter the name for the engagement file. You can also select the start date and end date for your engagement and enter budget information.

  4. In the Preparer field, enter your first name to search for your account. Select your account to assign yourself as the preparer.

  5. In the Reviewer field, enter part of another staff member’s name. Select their account to assign them as the reviewer.

  6. At the bottom of the OnPoint PCR dialog, select the Save button.

You have created your new engagement, and you have assigned staff in the firm to prepare the engagement and to review it. When you select Save, the engagement file is automatically opened in your browser.

Create a new engagement file

CaseWare Cloud’s Engagements page gives you a high-level view of all of the engagements currently being worked on by your organization. You can access the page from the Cloud menu.

On the Engagement page, you can easily review the status of the engagements, and you can select individual engagements to see its status and how much work is left on each.

To create a new OnPoint PCR engagement:

  1. Select the New button.

  2. In the New Engagement dialog, select OnPoint PCR.

  3. In the New OnPoint PCR dialog, choose your client entity and enter the name for the engagement file. You can also select the start date and end date for your engagement and enter budget information.

  4. Select Save.

Your new engagement is created. If you select All on the side panel to view all of the engagements for your organization, you will see the engagement added to the list.

Assign staff to the engagement

Once you have created the engagement file, you can assign staff members to work on it. Select the engagement to expand it. You can see engagement information such as budgets and key dates.

You can assign staff members to the engagement from here. Select the Assigned field (notice that no staff members have been assigned to this file as yet). A Manage Team popup is displayed. You can enter the name of one of your team members, and when you select their profile, you can assign the staff member as a preparer, a reviewer, or a second reviewer.

Assign yourself as a preparer, and assign other staff members as preparers and reviewers. Once you have assigned yourself to the engagement, you will see it displayed when you select My Work from the side panel. You will also see the initials of the staff members assigned to this engagement.

Share the file with your client contact

One of the benefits of OnPoint PCR is that you can easily collaborate with your client while working on the engagement. To enable this, your client needs to have a contact account on CaseWare Cloud, and you need to assign the appropriate role for this contact to access the engagement.

In Cloud, go to the detail page for the client’s entity. At the bottom of the details, in the Files section, you will see the new engagement for your client.

Select the file to open the detail dialog, and select the Share icon ().

In the Share dialog, enable contact access and then grant your client contact the Contact - Editor role.

This will enable your client to collaborate with your firm staff on the engagement file. They cannot open the file itself, but they will be able to see discussions and tasks, and they will be able to respond to client requests.

Set the engagement properties

Now that you have assigned the staff members and clients to the file, you’re ready to open the engagement file. Open the engagement from the Engagements page.

When you first open the engagement, a pop-up with information about the engagement file is displayed.

You can edit some of the high-level engagement information here:

  • Workflow stage - you can select a workflow stage to indicate the status of your engagement work.

  • Engagement details - you can enter a job number, change the operating name, and select a different year end date.

  • Flag a second reviewer - you can enable a second set of review sign-offs for the documents in the engagement. You would normally do this only once a first round of reviews is complete.

  • Lock down the file - you can lock down the engagement file once all engagement work is finalized.

Enter a job number and verify that your client’s year end date is correct, then save your changes.

Your engagement properties have now been saved, and you have completed all of the steps for initial engagement setup.
