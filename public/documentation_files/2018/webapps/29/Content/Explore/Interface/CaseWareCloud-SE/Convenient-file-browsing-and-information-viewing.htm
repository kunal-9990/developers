Convenient file browsing and information viewing (SE)

  • Locate documents
  • Display guidance for a procedure
  • Review prepared documents

This feature is only available with products on the CaseWareCloud SE platform.

Learn more about Smart Engagement products.

While working on your file, you will complete many work programs, create letters, and submit client requests. You can access documents related to each task on the left panel. Similar to the Documents page, the left panel allows you to access all your documents and groups them by engagement stage. Within these high-level groupings, documents are also organized into folders.

Locate documents

In the left panel, your documents are listed by engagement stage. When you open a document, your documents remain displayed in the panel for easy access.

Additionally, you can use the following features in the left panel to help you locate specific information or documents for your engagement work:

  • Search - Locate specific information in documents by typing keywords into the Search field. When you perform a search, the left panel displays all matching documents.
  • Recent - Access documents that you’ve opened recently. If you use one of the recent documents often while completing an engagement, you can select the pin icon () beside it to pin the document to the top of the panel for convenient access.
  • Document Map - Once you’ve opened a document, you may see a list of its sections on the Document Map, depending on the formatting applied to the document. You can select a section to jump directly to it. This saves you from having to scroll through a lengthy document, such as the financial statement, to view the relevant section.

You can open and close the left panel by selecting the arrow () at the bottom. Within the left panel, you can also expand and collapse the listed documents as well as the panel's other features by selecting the arrows () beside them.

Display guidance for a procedure

In an engagement file, you will typically record your engagement work in documents called checklists. Each checklist includes a series of tasks to guide you through the engagement work, and as you perform each task, you can enter responses or data and sign off on the task.

Each item in a checklist is called a procedure. Procedures are tasks you’ll need to complete or information you’ll need to gather. They often include fields for you to enter information or conclusions. You can display guidance for these procedures on the left panel.

To display guidance for a procedure:

Select the light bulb icon () located beside certain procedures to display guidance for them on the left panel. This guidance may present information that helps you to complete the procedure, or it may describe the procedure’s outcome.

Review prepared documents

From the left panel you can also access tools that make it easier to review an engagement. If you are reviewing a checklist, you can use these tools to highlight any of the following:

  • areas of your document that have been modified
  • responses that may indicate a problem with the engagement
  • parts of the document that are incomplete

To use the review tools:

At the top of the document, select the Review icon ().

Review tools are displayed in the left panel. Highlighted sections of your document need assessment.

In the left panel, you can enable or disable the different types of review content by selecting different checkboxes. You can filter the review areas to highlight the following types of content:

Content Type Description

Added

Review any content that was added by staff members. You can ensure that it is relevant and necessary for the engagement.

Modified

Review any content that staff members have modified. You can ensure that the changes are correct and that content still complies with guidelines and standards.

Overridden visibility

Review content that has been manually hidden or forced visible by staff members.

Optimized out

Review content that has been automatically hidden based on its visibility conditions. You can confirm that the hidden content does not apply to this engagement.

Non optimal answers

Review any responses that may indicate a problem on the engagement. You can confirm that the chosen response is correct, and you can determine whether annotation or supporting documentation is required.

Modified after sign-off

Review any responses that were changed after a staff member had signed off on the procedure.

Not signed off

Review any procedures that have not been signed off by staff members.

These tools apply visual markup to the document, making potential issues stand out on the page and enabling you to complete your review more quickly.
